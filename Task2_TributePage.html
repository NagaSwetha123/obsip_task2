Raja Ram Mohan Roy

Raja Ram Mohan Roy was a visionary social reformer and the father of the Indian Renaissance. He was born on May 22, 1772, in Bengal, India. Roy tirelessly campaigned against various social evils and fought for the rights of women, education, and religious reform.

One of the things I admire most about Raja Ram Mohan Roy is his progressive outlook towards society. He advocated for the abolition of practices such as sati and child marriage, which were prevalent during his time. His efforts led to the passage of the Sati Regulation Act in 1829, which banned the practice of sati.

Raja Ram Mohan Roy was also a staunch supporter of education for all, regardless of caste or gender. He believed that education was the key to social progress and worked towards the establishment of schools and colleges in India. His contributions laid the foundation for modern education in the country.

In addition to his social reform work, Raja Ram Mohan Roy was a prolific writer and thinker. He founded the Brahmo Sabha, which later evolved into the Brahmo Samaj, a reformist movement that aimed to promote monotheism and rationalism.

"The true test of civilization is not the census, nor the size of the cities, nor the crops, but the kind of man that the country turns out." - Raja Ram Mohan Roy
